# PROOF User Guide - Project Proposer

> For funded Catalyst proposers managing their projects

---

## Overview

As a funded Catalyst proposer, PROOF provides tools to:
- Claim ownership of your projects
- Submit monthly progress reports
- Respond to community concerns
- Build your accountability score

---

## Claiming Your Project

### Why Claim?

- **Verified ownership**: Badge shows you're the actual proposer
- **Submit reports**: Only claimed projects can receive monthly updates
- **Respond to concerns**: Address issues directly
- **Build reputation**: Your accountability score reflects your delivery

### How to Claim

1. **Find Your Project**
   - Search for your project in the directory
   - Navigate to the project detail page

2. **Initiate Claim**
   - Click **Claim This Project** button
   - Button appears only for unclaimed projects

3. **Verify Ownership**
   Choose one method:
   
   | Method | Process |
   |--------|---------|
   | **Wallet Signature** | Connect wallet used in proposal ‚Üí Sign verification message |
   | **Email Verification** | Verify email matches proposal submission |
   | **Manual Review** | Provide evidence to moderators |

4. **Wait for Approval**
   - Claims reviewed within 24-48 hours
   - You'll receive email notification
   - Upon approval, you're marked as project owner

### Multiple Team Members

- Multiple people can claim the same project
- Each person claims with their individual account
- Primary proposer automatically has edit rights
- Co-proposers can submit reports and respond to concerns

## Submitting Monthly Reports

### Why Submit Reports?

- **Transparency**: Community sees your progress
- **Accountability**: Builds your reputation score
- **Documentation**: Creates historical record
- **Engagement**: Shows active development

### Report Frequency

- Submit **once per month** while project is active
- Reports optional for completed projects
- Missed reports may affect accountability score

### How to Submit

1. Navigate to your claimed project page
2. Scroll to **Monthly Reports** section
3. Click **Submit Report**
4. Fill out the form:

| Field | Required | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Summary** | Yes | What was accomplished this month |
| **Blockers** | No | Any issues or challenges faced |
| **Next Steps** | Yes | Plans for next month |
| **Evidence URLs** | Recommended | Links to demos, screenshots, commits |

5. Click **Submit for Review**
6. Moderators review within 48 hours
7. Approved reports appear publicly on project page

### Writing Effective Reports

**Good Report Example:**
```
## Summary
Completed milestone 2: Smart contract audit. Deployed to testnet.
Successfully integrated with Cardano wallet connectors.

## Evidence
- Audit report: [link]
- Testnet deployment: [link]
- GitHub commits: [link]

## Blockers
Waiting on partner API integration - expected resolution next week.

## Next Steps
Begin milestone 3: Frontend development and user testing.
```

**Poor Report Example:**
```
Working on things. Making progress.
```

### Report Status Flow

```
Draft ‚Üí Submitted ‚Üí Under Review ‚Üí Approved/Changes Requested
```

If changes requested:
1. You'll receive notification with feedback
2. Edit and resubmit
3. Moderator re-reviews

## Responding to Concerns

### Why Respond?

- Shows engagement and accountability
- Addresses community questions
- Builds trust through transparency
- Improves your accountability score

### How to Respond

1. You'll receive notification when concern is filed
2. Navigate to project page ‚Üí Concerns section
3. Find the concern
4. Click **Respond**
5. Write your response:

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| ‚úÖ Address the specific issue | ‚ùå Be defensive or dismissive |
| ‚úÖ Provide evidence | ‚ùå Make excuses without action |
| ‚úÖ Explain timeline for resolution | ‚ùå Attack the person who raised it |
| ‚úÖ Acknowledge valid points | ‚ùå Ignore the concern |

6. Click **Submit Response**
7. Your response appears publicly below the concern

### Response Best Practices

**Example Good Response:**
```
Thank you for raising this. You're correct that Milestone 2 
was delivered 3 weeks late. This was due to unexpected smart 
contract audit findings that required additional development.

We've updated our timeline and Milestone 3 is now on track 
for completion by [date]. See our latest progress report for details.
```

**Example Poor Response:**
```
This isn't fair. We're working hard and you don't understand 
how difficult this is.
```

### Concern Resolution

If the concern is addressed:
1. The person who raised it can mark it resolved
2. Moderators can also close concerns
3. Resolved concerns show as "Addressed" with response

## Building Your Accountability Score

### How It's Calculated

Your score (0-100) combines:

| Component | Weight | How to Improve |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| **Completion** | 30% | Complete more projects you start |
| **Delivery** | 25% | Hit milestone deadlines |
| **Community** | 20% | Get positive reviews |
| **Efficiency** | 15% | Deliver value relative to funding |
| **Communication** | 10% | Submit reports, respond to concerns |

### Improving Your Score

1. **Complete milestones on time**: Single biggest factor
2. **Submit monthly reports**: Shows active communication
3. **Respond to concerns**: Demonstrates engagement
4. **Deliver quality**: Better reviews = higher score
5. **Finish projects**: Completion rate matters most

### Score Disputes

If you believe your score is incorrect:

1. Navigate to your profile
2. Click **Dispute Score**
3. Provide evidence of incorrect data
4. Moderators review and adjust if warranted
5. All disputes are logged for audit

## Managing Project Links

### Why Keep Links Updated?

- Helps community verify your progress
- Improves your project's visibility
- Broken links hurt credibility

### Updating Links

1. Navigate to your claimed project
2. Click **Edit Links** (if you're the owner)
3. Add/update:
   - GitHub repository URL
   - Project website
   - Documentation
   - Social media accounts
   - Demo/testnet links

## Tips for Success

### Before Submitting a Proposal

- Research similar past proposals on PROOF
- Check proposers' accountability scores
- Understand what delivery looks like

### During Development

- Submit monthly reports even when progress is slow
- Address concerns promptly
- Keep all links and documentation current
- Engage with community questions

### After Completion

- Update project status to completed
- Ensure all milestone evidence is linked
- Request reviews from users of your project
- Your score will reflect successful delivery

## Common Questions

### What if I have multiple projects?

Each project is tracked separately. Your overall accountability score is an aggregate across all projects.

### What if my project is delayed?

Delays happen. What matters is:
1. Communicate proactively
2. Submit reports explaining blockers
3. Provide updated timelines
4. Don't disappear

### Can I remove a negative review?

No. Reviews cannot be removed by project owners. You can:
- Respond to the review with your perspective
- Report if it violates guidelines (false claims, harassment)
- Moderators can remove policy-violating content

### What if someone else claims my project?

Fraudulent claims are reviewed by moderators. Provide evidence of your ownership (wallet signature, email, etc.) and the fraudulent claim will be rejected.
